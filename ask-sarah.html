I keep getting generic answers from Claude. What am I doing wrong?
Ask Sarah
The most common reason is not enough context. Claude is incredibly good but it only knows what you tell it.

Try this: Before your question, add two or three sentences about who you are, what you do, and what you are trying to achieve. Then be specific about the format and tone you want.

Instead of "write me a LinkedIn post" try "I am a marketing consultant who works with B2B SaaS companies. Write a LinkedIn post about why most businesses waste their AI budget. Conversational tone, no hashtags, under 150 words."

The output will be completely different.
That makes so much sense. What about for research tasks?
Ask Sarah
For research I actually switch to Perplexity rather than Claude. It searches the live internet and shows you the sources so you can verify everything. Claude's knowledge has a cutoff date so for anything current, Perplexity is a much better starting point.
